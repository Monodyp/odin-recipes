Sinigang

The perfect ulam to have in the cold, rainy weather. As the army of water droplets drop over your home, dazzling your environment in a chilly atmospehere, the warmth and hearth of the Sinigang invokes a blissful feeling to the soul.

Ingredients

Below are the ingredients you need to cook your own delicious Hearthy Sinigang

  • 1 kilo Pork/pork ribs, cut into bite-size chunks
  • 4 tomatoes, quartered
  • 3 medium sized onions, halved
  • 4 pieces gabi (taro), peeled
  • Sampalok (tamarind) mix (Use the "with Gabi" variant of you don't have fresh gabi) Patis (fish sauce) or salt 1 liter of rice wash (1st batch of water used to wash rice) or plain water (adjust if needed) 2 "bundles" kangkong (you can use more bundle if you want)
  • 1 bundle String beans
  • 3 pieces radish, sliced
  • 2-3 pieces sili pansigang (green finger pepper)
  • 10 pcs small sized Okra
  • Egg plant, sliced

Procedures

Below are the following steps to take the ingredients above, and perfectly concoct the delicious dish that is Sinigang

  1. In a pot with water or rice wash, boil pork, tomatoes, onions, gabi until the pork and gabi becomes tender.
  2. Add the sinigang sa sampalok mix, labanos, okra, string beans, eggplant and siling pansigang. Season with fish sauce or salt
  3. Adjust the sourness by adding more mix or water.
  4. When labanos/okra are tender, add the kangkong tops and simmer for a minute more or two.
